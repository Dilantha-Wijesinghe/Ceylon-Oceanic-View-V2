Nilaveli

Some people consider this beach the best in Sri Lanka, and not without reasons. If you want to enjoy calmness and solitude, spend time along in quietness, then come to this uncrowded but extremely beautiful Nilaveli Beach. You will be excited by the breathtaking landscapes and outside exotica both on the beach and on your way to it.

The beach is located in the eastern part of the country about 120 kilometers from Colombo and 14 kilometers to the south of Trincomalee Beach. It is a vast location covered in white sand, with its coast merging with clear blue water. It offers great conditions for children and tourist swimming who may not feel comfortable in the water. The sea bottom, like the beach itself, is even, with stones being very rare. Even if you go far from the shore your feet are still visible, which says a lot about the water clearness despite the sandy beach.

The occasional waves can be encountered (it is the ocean after all), but they aren't high. They're also "soft" and don't knock people down, unlike the waves at Hikkaduwa. The beach is mostly empty, which makes it attracting for some.

Pigeon Island, a few hundred meters offshore from Nilaveli Beach is a great spot for snorkelling. Diving is also possible a little further offshore, where the coral reefs have a great display of underwater life.

The best time to visit is during late August and early September as the rain starts to set by mid-October

Trincomalee

Trincomalee is located in the northeast part of Sri Lanka, within the port town of the same name. Trincomalee is the most famous resort of Sri Lanka, visited every year by thousands of tourists from all over the world. Visitors are attracted by the endless coastline covered in white sand, clean transparent and unique turquoise sea and rich underwater world in the beach's surroundings. The gigantic bay is protected from strong winds and ocean currents, and the bay waters are calm and safe. The sun shines all year long here, and long rains are very rare.

The city beach borders Cape Konesar Malai, with an ancient hindu temple atop of it, in the south. The entrance is free, there are several cafes and snack bars that offer the guests umbrellas and sunbeds when they order food and beverages. You can partake in windsurfing, ride water attractions, watch local fishermen and buy the newly caught fish from them here.

Trincomalee has many wrecks as well as reefs that make it a sought after diving destination. The HMS Diomede was a 44-gun fifth rate built during the American Revolutionary Wars. She was deployed in a squadron to Ceylon to take Trincomalee from the Dutch. However, coming into the bay, she struck a sunken rock and sank off the coast of Trincomalee in August 1795. The dive site is silty and visibility is not great. Objects found at the site are large cannons and anchors. The site is at a depth of 42 meters.

The best time to embark on whale watching tours in Trincomalee is between the months of May and September. December, January and April are the best months to enjoy a whale watching expedition in the country.

Marble Beach

Marble beach, also known as Marble Bay is one of the beautiful beaches in Trincomalee. Because it's 17 kilometres outside of Trincomalee located, not many people know about this place. Run by the air force, there is a resort and restaurant at the north end of the cove of Marble beach. Spend your time at the south end of the beach in beautiful emerald water and enjoy the quiet area. In our opinion this is one of the best beaches in Sri Lanka.

However, the highlight of the beach is its fine sand and the sightings of peacocks, monkeys and cattle along with a variety of birds. For the lazy visitor, the sands are perfect for spending a few hours at the beach getting a sun-kissed tan. When here, don't forget to visit the nearby 'Diamond Hill' that offers a fabulous view of the city.

Besides enjoying the beach, sunbathing and swimming, you can go snorkeling around the rocks at Marble beach. There are some colourful fish to spot here, and because the water is so calm you don't have to worry about the current or big waves. If you're hungry, the restaurant at the beach serves delicious foods.If you want to go exploring around, then there are plenty of things to do in Trincomalee.

The best time to visit is during late August and early September as the rain starts to set by mid-October
